Correlations

Hello, let's explore some Correlations. First let's explain what the heck correlations are. Correlation - "A mutual relationship or connection between two or more things." We will be looking at Pearson's correlation coefficient. If your lost already don't worry, I was too.

Pearson's correlation coefficient attempts to draw a line of the best fit through the data of two variables, and the Pearson correlation coefficient is a measure of linear relationship between the variables. The stronger the association of the two variables, the closer the Pearson correlation coefficient will be to either +1 or -1 depending on whether the relationship is positive or negative.

Achieving a value of +1 or -1 means that all your data points are included on the line of best fit, there are no data points that show any variation away from this line. The closer the value to 0 the greater the variation between the two variables.

For the majority of our research we are going to be comparing Bitcoin vs other assets as our two variables The correlation coefficient is calculated by taking the covariance of two assets and then dividing that by the product of their standard deviations (volatility).

DISCLAIMER : One thing to note - Outliers can effect the correlations but let's not get into that. First we will use a scatter plot, followed by a Bland-Altman plot.
